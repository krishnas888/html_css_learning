Puliyogare

Ingredients:
  • Rice
  • Tamarind
  • Oil
  • Dry red chilies
  • Mustard seeds
Process:

To make Puliyogare, start by cooking 1 cup of rice and letting it cool. Ensure the rice grains are separate and not mushy. In a small bowl, soak a lemon-sized ball of tamarind in warm water for 10 minutes, then extract the pulp and set it aside.

Next, heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan over medium flame. Add 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds and let them splutter. Lower the flame and add 4-5 dry red chilies, 1 teaspoon of chana dal, 1 teaspoon of urad dal, and a pinch of asafoetida. Sauté until the dals turn golden brown.

Now, add the tamarind pulp, 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 tablespoon of jaggery, and salt to taste. Cook on low flame for about 5-7 minutes until the tamarind mixture thickens and the oil starts to separate from it.

Add the cooled rice to the pan and mix gently to coat the rice with the tamarind mixture. Adjust salt if needed. Cook on a low flame for another 2 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend. Serve Puliyogare warm, garnished with roasted peanuts or cashews.

Paneer Butter Masala

Ingredients:
  • Paneer
  • Tomatoes
  • Butter
  • Spices
    • cumin
    • red chili powder
    • garam masala
Process:

Begin by cutting 250 grams of paneer into cubes and setting it aside. Puree 4 large tomatoes and set them aside as well. Heat 2 tablespoons of butter in a pan over medium flame. Add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds and let them splutter.

Lower the flame and add the tomato puree. Cook for 10-12 minutes on medium flame, stirring occasionally until the puree thickens and the oil separates from the tomato mixture. Add 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, 1 teaspoon of garam masala, and salt to taste. Stir well.

Add the paneer cubes and 1/2 cup of fresh cream. Mix gently and let it simmer on low flame for about 5 minutes, allowing the paneer to absorb the flavors. If the gravy is too thick, add a little water to achieve the desired consistency.

Serve Paneer Butter Masala hot, garnished with a drizzle of cream and a sprinkle of fresh coriander leaves. This dish pairs well with naan or rice.

Aloo Gobi

Ingredients:
  • Potatoes
  • Cauliflower
  • Spices
    • turmeric
    • cumin
    • coriander
    • powder
  • Oil
Process:

Start by peeling and chopping 2 large potatoes into cubes. Cut a medium-sized cauliflower into florets and wash them thoroughly. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan over medium flame. Add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds and let them sizzle.

Lower the flame and add 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder, followed by the chopped potatoes and cauliflower florets. Stir to coat the vegetables with the turmeric and oil. Add 1 teaspoon of coriander powder, 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, and salt to taste. Mix well.

Cover the pan with a lid and cook on low flame for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. The vegetables should become tender but not mushy. If needed, sprinkle a little water to prevent sticking and ensure even cooking.

Once the vegetables are cooked through, remove the lid and cook on medium flame for another 2-3 minutes to let any excess moisture evaporate. Serve Aloo Gobi hot, garnished with chopped fresh coriander leaves. It is a perfect side dish for roti or rice.

Chole

Ingredients:
  • Chickpeas
  • Tomatoes
  • Onions
  • Spices
    • garam masala
    • cumin
    • coriander powder
Process:

Soak 1 cup of chickpeas overnight or for at least 8 hours. Drain and rinse them before pressure cooking with 3 cups of water and a pinch of salt until tender (about 6-7 whistles). Set aside the cooked chickpeas.

Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan over medium flame. Add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add 2 finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Lower the flame and add 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste. Cook for 2-3 minutes until the raw smell disappears.

Add 3 pureed tomatoes and cook on medium flame for 10 minutes until the oil separates from the mixture. Add 1 teaspoon of garam masala, 1 teaspoon of coriander powder, 1 teaspoon of cumin powder, and 1 teaspoon of red chili powder. Stir well and cook for another 2 minutes.

Add the cooked chickpeas along with the cooking water to the pan. Mix well and let it simmer on low flame for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together. Adjust salt and spice levels if needed. Serve Chole hot, garnished with chopped coriander leaves and a squeeze of lemon juice. Enjoy with bhature, rice, or roti.

Vegetable Biryani

Ingredients:
  • Basmati rice
  • Mixed vegetables
    1. carrots
    2. peas
    3. beans
    4. potatoes
  • Spices
    1. bay leaf
    2. cinnamon
    3. cloves
    4. cardamom
  • Yogurt
Process:

Rinse 1 cup of basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil or ghee in a large pot over medium flame. Add 1 bay leaf, 1-inch cinnamon stick, 4 cloves, and 2 cardamom pods. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.

Lower the flame and add 1 finely chopped onion. Sauté until golden brown. Add 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste and cook for 2-3 minutes until the raw smell disappears. Add 2 finely chopped tomatoes and cook on medium flame until they turn mushy.

Add 1 cup of mixed vegetables (chopped carrots, peas, beans, and potatoes) and 1/2 cup of yogurt. Mix well and cook for 5 minutes on medium flame. Add 1 teaspoon of biryani masala, 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, and salt to taste. Stir well.

Add the soaked and drained rice to the pot. Mix gently to combine. Add 2 cups of water and bring it to a boil on high flame. Once it starts boiling, lower the flame to the lowest setting, cover the pot with a tight lid, and cook for 15-20 minutes until the rice is fully cooked and the water is absorbed.

Turn off the flame and let the biryani rest for 10 minutes before fluffing it with a fork. Serve Vegetable Biryani hot, garnished with fried onions, chopped mint, and coriander leaves. Enjoy with raita or a side salad.
